The Catfish's Habitat

Catfish are a highly adaptable species found in a wide range of aquatic habitats worldwide.
They thrive in freshwater environments, including rivers, lakes, ponds, and swamps. Their
habitat preference can vary depending on the specific species, but most catfish are bottom-dwellers,
making them well-suited to muddy or sandy substrates. Many species prefer slow-moving or still waters
with plenty of hiding spots like submerged logs, rocks, and vegetation. Catfish are known for their nocturnal
behavior, often venturing out to feed during the cover of darkness. Their ability to live in diverse
habitats and adapt to changing environmental conditions contributes to their success as a species.
